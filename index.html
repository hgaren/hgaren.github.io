Garen Haddeler

I am currently pursuing a PhD at the National University of Singapore (NUS) under the guidance of Prof Chee-Meng Chew. Additionally, I am engaged as a student researcher with the Robotics and Autonomous Systems (RAS) department at the Institute for Infocomm Research (I2R) in the Agency for Science, Technology, and Research (A*STAR), collaborating with Dr. Albertus Hendrawan Adiwahono and Dr. Micheal Chuah.

Over the last four years, my research has primarily revolved around terrain sensing, terrain mapping, navigation, decision-making strategies for legged robots, and the development of control algorithms tailored for legged robots operating in non-rigid grounds and outdoor environments. This includes addressing challenging conditions such as pitfall areas concealed by grass and obstructed vision systems due to long grasses.

Prior to my current pursuits, I have completed my bachelor's degree in Control and Automation Engineering from Istanbul Technical University (ITU) in 2019. During my undergraduate years, I worked as a researcher in the Robotic Laboratory, where Prof Hakan Temeltas provided guidance. Additionally, I actively participated in extracurricular activities, serving as a member of the software developing subteam in the ITU Rover team . Within this team, we focused on developing control algorithms for a robotic arm in 2017 and autonomous navigation in 2018 and attended competitions named University Rover Challenge (URC 2017-2018) and European Rover Challenge (ERC 2018).

I am generally interested in Robotics, Computer Vision and Machine Learning with a specific focus on perception, navigation and control to enhance robot autonomy.
